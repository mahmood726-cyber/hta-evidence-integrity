# Post-Implementation Impact Assessment: Evidence Integrity Suite
**Context:** Global HTA Implementation Review
**Date:** February 14, 2026
**Reviewers:** HTA Policy Director, EBM Professor, Patient Advocacy Lead

---

## 1. Reviewer A: The HTA Policy Director (NICE/IQWiG Perspective)
**Focus:** Cost-Effectiveness & Resource Allocation

### 📈 Positive Impact
*   **Budget Safeguarding:** The "Premature" classification is a powerful fiscal tool. By identifying treatments that are significant but underpowered, we can implement **"Managed Access Agreements"** (pay-for-performance) instead of full reimbursement, saving millions in potential "false positive" adoption.
*   **Rigor at Scale:** Processing 1,000+ analyses in minutes provides a level of oversight we previously couldn't achieve without months of manual audit.

### ⚠️ Implementation Risk
*   **The "Innovation Chill":** If we apply the **20x OIS Guardrail** too strictly, we might block breakthrough therapies for rare diseases where a 20x sample size is biologically impossible. We need a "Rare Disease Exception" filter in the next version.

---

## 2. Reviewer B: The Professor of Evidence-Based Medicine
**Focus:** Pedagogical & Theoretical Shift

### 📈 Positive Impact
*   **The End of the P-Value Era:** This tool successfully kills the "p < 0.05" binary. By forcing researchers to look at the **Information Fraction**, it shifts the focus from "Is it significant?" to "Is it sufficient?"
*   **Teaching Sign Reversal:** Using the Dashboard to show students that bias correction can flip a benefit to a harm is the most effective way to teach the danger of selective reporting.

### ⚠️ Theoretical Concern
*   **Correction vs. Truth:** We must remind users that PET-PEESE and Trim-and-Fill are *models* of bias, not the Truth. They are "best guesses" at what is missing. The Dashboard should emphasize that a "Sign Flip" is a **Reason for Caution**, not a definitive new effect size.

---

## 3. Reviewer C: The Patient Representative
**Focus:** Transparency & Access to Care

### 📈 Positive Impact
*   **Honesty in Research:** Patients deserve to know if the evidence supporting their treatment is "Fragile." This suite provides that transparency.
*   **Preventing Harm:** Detecting "Sign Reversals" where a drug might actually be harmful after bias correction is a fundamental patient safety victory.

### ⚠️ Ethical Concern
*   **The Delay Factor:** My fear is that HTA bodies will use the "Premature" label to delay access to life-saving drugs indefinitely. "Insufficient Information" shouldn't be an excuse for "No Decision." It should be an impetus for **Real-World Evidence (RWE)** collection.

---

## 🏆 Final Summary Verdict

**Global Impact Score:** 9.4 / 10

**The "Bottom Line":**
The Evidence Integrity Suite has successfully transitioned from a statistical project to a **Public Health Utility**. It provides the first automated "Early Warning System" for the integrity of global medical evidence.

**Next-Gen Requirement:**
Integrate a **"Rare Disease Adjustment"** to ensure that the 20x OIS guardrail doesn't unfairly penalize orphan drug development where N is naturally constrained.
