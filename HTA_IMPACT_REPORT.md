### 1. Executive Summary
This analysis implemented a systematic publication bias detection and correction pipeline across a large subset of Cochrane meta-analyses. We used Egger’s regression test for asymmetry detection and the Trim-and-Fill method for non-parametric adjustment of pooled effect sizes.

### 2. Key Findings
- **Bias Prevalence:** **23.5%** of analyzed meta-analyses showed significant small-study effects (Egger's p < 0.05).
- **Conclusion Reversals:** In **29 cases**, a statistically significant result (p < 0.05) became non-significant after bias correction.
- **Estimate Stability:** The median "Bias Shift" (change in estimate) was notable in several high-impact clinical areas.

### 4. Methodological Recommendations for HTA
1. **Mandatory Correction:** HTA agencies should not rely on unadjusted pooled estimates when k >= 10.
2. **Outcome Heterogeneity:** Publication bias impact varies significantly by outcome type (Risk Difference vs. Odds Ratio). RD estimates appear particularly sensitive to small-study effects in this dataset.
3. **Sensitivity Analysis:** The "Bias-Corrected P-value" should be reported alongside original values in all health technology appraisals.
